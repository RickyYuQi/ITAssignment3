Collective Reflection

With the completion of this assignment, it has come to our attention that, overall, the group worked relatively well together. This being a first year first semester course, for most of the members in the group this would have been their first major assignment. Which forces them into the situation of working together with many people they are unfamiliar with.

The main positives of team SPURS were that the quality of work produced was deemed satisfactory by the group, though there were many aspects of the project that could have been better performed. In particular in the IT work section, it would have been beneficial for the group to have done an interview with an IT professional instead of just referencing ten articles, as it would have introduced us to a real professional and it would have given us experience with interacting in a work environment. When it came time to divide the workload and split the members into mini groups, the teamwork and input of most members was relatively evenly distributed. There were of course some members who went above and beyond, putting in more input into this project than others. Though not always productive, it was discovered that the group could work at a quick and efficient pace when properly motivated. Whenever a group member had trouble with collecting research or completing their section of the assignment, other members would help them understand the possible problem and resolve the misunderstanding. One of the major positives of our group was that the group would consistently hold the two Microsoft Teams meetings per week requirement, with an average participation with most of the group members. Though the discussion was not always relevant, the environment in the meetings was very approachable and the group did not have any major disagreements.

The main negatives of team SPURS were that our time management skills were quite poor, even with having consistent meetings and a proper timeline to schedule the project’s completion, the group would consistently not complete work by the initial deadline. Whilst, not with all members there was a lack of communication with some of the members, not attending the group meetings or even not contacting the group for days at a time. Lastly, most probably the largest negative of working with the group would have been a complete lack of productivity during the initial first two weeks of the assignment, had the group worked properly and productively the work quality would have most likely been much higher given the larger time constraint.
